ROAS - Measurement and Evaluation Context
"Estimated cumulative Robux earnings (excluding subscriptions, engagement payouts, and immersive ads) within 30 days of users playing through your ad. Robux revenue is delayed by up to 3 days, and will continue updating for up to 33 days after your campaign ends as users continue spending." - Roblox Ads Manager
  • Wave 1 objective - core loop validation, not revenue: Early campaign waves are structured to drive initial player volume to test whether the core game loop retains players. Revenue follows retention - a game that keeps players will eventually monetise; one that does not will not, regardless of ROAS. We deliberately prioritise engagement metrics (D1, D7, session time, NUFSR) at this stage.
  • ROAS improves as the loop strengthens: Players typically do not spend in their first session. Revenue comes on the 2nd-3rd visit or after progression milestones are reached. 30-day ROAS will read low in early waves - this is structural, not a failure signal.
  • ROAS is a lagging indicator of game health, not a campaign success gate. It is monitored to track monetisation trajectory across waves, not to evaluate individual campaign performance. Primary KPIs at this stage: D1, D7, session time, NUFSR - these proxy LTV.

Key funnel insights:
  • Mid-training drop (30% exit): training may be too long or unclear. Shortening to core mechanics is the highest-priority fix.
  • Post-training to first match (35% exit): players finishing training are not finding the path into a match. A clear CTA after training is needed.
  • Second match rate: 0.033% - the most critical failure. No hook after match 1 to pull players back.
  • The in-game upgrade system is actively used by players who persist - the core mechanics work once engaged. The loop breaks before they return for a second session.
  • Root cause of both low D1 retention and the 2.5-minute average session time.
